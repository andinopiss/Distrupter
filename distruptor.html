About Me

Distruptor

"Disrupter" is not your typical college project. It's a unique combination of robotics, wireless communication, and a touch of mischief, all rolled into one captivating rover. Here's a quick glimpse of what this project entails:

Rover Control with Raspberry Pi: We've harnessed the power of a Raspberry Pi running Ubuntu Server to remotely control our rover. This allows us to navigate it with precision and ease.

Signal Jammer with ESP8266: The rover features an ESP8266 module, known for its wireless capabilities. With it, we can temporarily disrupt wireless connections within a specified range.

Bad USB with Raspberry Pi Pico: The Raspberry Pi Pico serves as the mischievous sidekick, a "bad USB" capable of executing amusing pranks on a connected computer. It simulates keyboard inputs to create moments of hilarity.

Our project is not just about technology; it's about exploring the boundaries of creativity, ethics, and responsible usage. We emphasize the importance of using this technology responsibly and respecting the privacy and consent of individuals in any interactions. So, join me on this exciting journey as we delve into the world of disruptive innovation and ethical exploration. Together, we'll learn, create, and perhaps even share a few laughs along the way. Feel free to explore our project, ask questions, and discover the potential of combining technology with a dash of humor.
